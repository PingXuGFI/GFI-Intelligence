External Analysis

What GL offers as an external research framework

Government audit offices generate the data. GL applies an external formula to that data to produce cross-jurisdictional comparisons and structural efficiency scores.

  • External research perspective: GL produces cross-jurisdictional structural comparisons using publicly available data — analysis that complements the work of individual state offices.
  • Cross-state comparison: GL applies the same formula across jurisdictions, producing comparative efficiency scores that no single state office can generate.
  • Political neutrality: GL does not evaluate policy goals. It only measures whether a system accomplished what it was designed to accomplish — a question that applies equally regardless of political context.
  • No internal documents required: GL analysis is based entirely on publicly available state audit records. No confidential data, no internal systems access, no compliance exposure.
GL Applied · International

Same Framework. Nine Countries.

GL scores are derived from publicly available governance and administrative efficiency data. These are independent research applications of the GL formula — not commissioned engagements with any government.

EE
Estonia · GL Score 10.54
Digital-First Design Aligns Speed with Accuracy

Estonia's public administration is consistently cited in OECD and EU governance reports for high e-service adoption rates and low administrative redundancy. GL analysis of available governance indicators produces the highest score in this research set.

GL reading: when the denominator — total administrative steps required to deliver a valid outcome — is designed into the digital architecture from the start, friction does not accumulate. Estonia illustrates what a low-friction denominator looks like in practice.

NZ
New Zealand · GL Score 7.21
Outcome Measurement Built Into Delivery

New Zealand's public service reforms introduced outcome-based performance measurement across government agencies. Published government performance reports document service delivery metrics alongside user experience indicators.

GL reading: systems that measure outputs and outcomes simultaneously give administrators the data needed to locate denominator problems before they accumulate. New Zealand's framework illustrates how measurement design affects structural efficiency.

SG
Singapore · GL Score 6.88
Centralized Process Logic Reduces Rework

Singapore's public sector efficiency is documented across multiple international governance indices including the World Bank Government Effectiveness indicators. Centralized service delivery architecture reduces inter-agency friction.

GL reading: when process logic is centralized, denominator inflation — the accumulation of redundant steps across agency boundaries — is structurally limited. Singapore illustrates how architecture choices determine friction levels independently of staffing or funding.

DE
Germany · GL Score 0.056
High Input, Low Flow — The Complexity Trap

Germany's public administration is characterized by high procedural compliance requirements documented across EU administrative burden assessments. Despite significant public sector investment, service delivery speed consistently lags comparable economies in international benchmarks.

GL reading: Germany illustrates what GL identifies as the complexity trap — when procedural requirements multiply the denominator faster than resources can address it, additional investment produces diminishing returns. High input does not resolve a misaligned denominator.

Methodology

GL measures process, not policy.

GL does not evaluate whether a policy goal is correct. It applies a structural formula to ask: given the stated goal, how efficiently does the system accomplish it? The same formula applies regardless of jurisdiction, program type, or political context.
