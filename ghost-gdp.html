GL Framework · Structural Friction Analysis

GDP counted it.
Nobody received it.
That's Ghost GDP.

Rework, waiting, re-filing, corrections — these activities register as economic output but deliver no real value. The GL Framework measures exactly how much of a process's intended value is absorbed by structural friction.

GL = (Fs × Vn) / (Pd × Cf)
Ghost GDP % = friction cost / total process cost
When GL < 1 → Ghost GDP is forming
The Problem
Output that reaches no one

Standard economic measurement counts activity — not delivery. Every hour spent re-filing a rejected application, waiting on hold, correcting a system error, or navigating an unclear form is counted as productive output. But none of it reached the person who needed it.

This is Ghost GDP: economic activity that appears in the numerator of productivity calculations while functioning as pure denominator cost for the people experiencing it.

The GL Connection
The same formula detects both

The GL Framework was designed to measure structural friction in governance and enterprise systems. Its denominator — Pd × Cf (Pain Duration × Cognitive Friction) — is precisely the component that generates Ghost GDP.

Ghost GDP % = (Pd × Cf) / [(Fs × Vn) + (Pd × Cf)]
When GL is low, this ratio is high. The same friction that collapses GL inflates Ghost GDP.
