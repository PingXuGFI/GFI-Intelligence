Methodological Framework

GFI applies a structured diagnostic protocol to evaluate whether automation strengthens or weakens structural legitimacy.

Phase 1 — Structural Mapping
Identify execution pathways, decision nodes, control layers, and escalation loops.

Phase 2 — Denominator Stress Measurement
Quantify process density (Pd), control friction (Cf), and systemic risk amplification (Srf).

Phase 3 — Legitimacy Calculation
Apply the Governance Legitimacy Formula.

Phase 4 — Capital Impact Modeling
Estimate friction absorption velocity and projected capital erosion under current structural conditions.

Phase 5 — Executive Interpretation
Translate findings into board-level risk classification and intervention priority.

Principle: GFI does not assume automation is beneficial. It verifies whether structural conditions support sustainable execution.

GL = (Fs × Vn) / (Pd × Cf × Srf)

Fs = Flow Stability · Vn = Value Nodes · Pd = Process Density · Cf = Control Friction · Srf = Systemic Risk Factor

第三階段 — 合法性計算
套用治理合法性公式。

第四階段 — 資本影響模型

第五階段 — 高階決策解讀
將結構結果轉化為董事會層級的風險分類與干預優先順序。

原則：GFI 不預設自動化是正確方向，而是驗證其是否符合結構可持續性。

Fs＝流程穩定度 · Vn＝價值節點 · Pd＝流程密度 · Cf＝控制摩擦 · Srf＝系統性風險因子
